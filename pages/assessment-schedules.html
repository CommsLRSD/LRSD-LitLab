Assessment Schedules

Recommended assessment schedules for universal screening and progress monitoring.

Universal Screening

Universal screening should be conducted for all students to identify those who may be at risk for reading difficulties.

Assessment Period Timeframe Purpose Students Assessed
Fall Screening First 3 weeks of school Identify students at risk and establish baseline data All students
Winter Screening Mid-January to early February Monitor student progress and adjust interventions All students
Spring Screening April to early May Evaluate year-end growth and plan for next year All students

Progress Monitoring

Progress monitoring is conducted more frequently for students receiving interventions to ensure the interventions are effective.

Tier Level Frequency Purpose
Tier 1 3 times per year (Universal Screening) Monitor general classroom instruction effectiveness
Tier 2 Bi-weekly to monthly Monitor intervention effectiveness and adjust as needed
Tier 3 Weekly Closely monitor intensive intervention and make frequent adjustments

Best Practices

  • Administer assessments in a quiet, distraction-free environment
  • Ensure assessors are properly trained on administration procedures
  • Complete all universal screening within the designated timeframe
  • Review data promptly and make instructional decisions within 1-2 weeks
  • Use multiple data points to make instructional decisions
  • Communicate results with families and involve them in the intervention process
