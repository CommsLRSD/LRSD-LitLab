Understanding Scores & Percentiles

Assessment Scores

Assessment scores represent a student's performance on specific literacy tasks. Scores are compared to grade-level benchmarks.

  • Blue Strong performance, skill demonstrated
  • Green Adequate performance, skill present
  • Yellow Below benchmark, skill needs attention
  • Red Well below benchmark, immediate intervention needed

Percentiles

Percentiles indicate how a student's performance compares to other students nationally.

  • 90th+ percentile: Exceeding expectations
  • 40th-90th percentile: Meeting grade-level expectations
  • 20th-39th percentile: Some risk, needs monitoring
  • Below 20th percentile: At risk, needs intervention

Frequently Asked Questions

DIBELS Assessments

DIBELS (Dynamic Indicators of Basic Early Literacy Skills) is a set of procedures and measures for assessing the acquisition of literacy skills from kindergarten through sixth grade.

DIBELS assessments are designed to be brief. Individual subtests typically take 1-3 minutes each. A complete screening battery usually takes 10-15 minutes per student.

CTOPP-2 Assessments

CTOPP-2 (Comprehensive Test of Phonological Processing, Second Edition) assesses phonological awareness, phonological memory, and rapid naming.

General Questions

Response to Intervention (RTI) and Multi-Tiered System of Supports (MTSS) are frameworks for providing academic support through increasingly intensive levels of instruction.

Interventions should be implemented with fidelity for at least 6-8 weeks before making decisions about effectiveness.

Tier 1: Whole class. Tier 2: Small groups of 3-6 students. Tier 3: Groups of 1-3 students or individual instruction.
